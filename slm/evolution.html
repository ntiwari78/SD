1Evolution timeline (2022 → 2026)

Five eras, each redefining what "small" means. Expanded from v1 to include the 2026 frontier (Gemma 4 PLE/p-RoPE, Llama 4 MoE, on-policy distillation, LoRA theory via Matrix Product States).
Era I · 2022 (13 papers)

Openness & Alignment

  • Open weights (OPT, BLOOM); SFT+RLHF formalised (InstructGPT); TRL ships.
  • PEFT seeded: BitFit, LoRA, P-Tuning v2.
  • Chinchilla empirically justifies the entire SLM direction.
  • FLAN-T5, Super-NaturalInstructions, Fine-tune-CoT bootstrap instruction tuning for small models.
Era II · 2023 (28 papers)

Data & Quantization

  • Phi-1 / Phi-1.5 / Phi-2 prove textbook-quality data beats scale.
  • QLoRA makes 65B finetuning fit on one GPU.
  • Orca / Orca 2, Distilling Step-by-Step, SCoTD, SCOTT: CoT → student.
  • LLaMA 2, Mistral 7B, Zephyr, LIMA, Alpaca, Vicuna open the ecosystem. LongLoRA enables 100K context PEFT.
Era III · 2024 (20 papers)

Architecture & Edge

  • TinyLlama, MobileLLM, OpenELM, Stable LM 2 push sub-2B on-device.
  • Phi-3, Llama 3.2 1B/3B, Gemma 2, Qwen2/2.5, MiniCPM set the 2024 baselines.
  • PEFT matures: DoRA (ICML Best Paper), MiLoRA, ReFT (15–65× fewer params).
  • Self-Refine IT, SGFT, FinQA PoT: SFT meets DPO + program synthesis.
Era IV · 2025 (23 papers)

Reasoning & Multimodal

  • DeepSeek-R1 (GRPO) distils RL-induced CoT into 1.5B–7B.
  • Phi-4-Mini (Mixture-of-LoRAs) + Phi-4-Mini-Reasoning (Rollout DPO).
  • Qwen3 thinking/non-thinking modes; Llama 4 MoE with 10M context.
  • Meerkat, MedGemma, Shakti, Tool-Call SLM; SLM-Bench formalises correctness + efficiency + sustainability.
Era V · 2026 (8+ papers)

Effective-Parameter & Open Frontier

  • Gemma 4 ships under Apache 2.0 (no MAU caps) — E2B/E4B/26B-A4B/31B.
  • Per-Layer Embeddings (PLE) + p-RoPE + hybrid K/V unify effective-param design.
  • On-policy distillation (Tencent HY-MT1.5) bridges 1.8B ↔ 7B teacher.
  • Artificial Entanglement (Matrix Product States) gives LoRA its first theoretical framework.
  • Production-grade small FT: LFM2.5-1.2B for systematic review screening; MedGemma Malaysia CPGs.

3Drawbacks & common failure modes

Grouped by category. Several 2024–2026 drawbacks surface from the expanded v2 coverage (toxicity shifts, licensing, systematic review limits, teacher dependency).

Multi-turn agentic stability

Sub-1B models drop sharply on multi-turn function-calling (BFCL). Even 1B–3B models cap around 65% pass rate on live tool-use with Hybrid SFT+DPO.

Style-mimicry without logic

Naïve CoT students copy the form of teacher reasoning without the reasoning. SCOTT, Rollout DPO, Meerkat only partially fix this; on-policy distillation helps but isn't universal yet.

Hallucinations under narrow domains

Domain-adapted SLMs still fabricate outside their training distribution. Honest AI (RAG refusal) and Neural-Symbolic Collaborative Distillation are early remedies.

Catastrophic forgetting after fine-tune

Full-parameter FT narrows the model onto the target domain; cheap PEFT helps but adds its own capacity limits. The 2024 Toxicity study shows even benign LoRA FT can drastically shift safety behaviour.

Teacher dependency

SOTA pipelines still rely on GPT-4 / PaLM-540B / Behemoth teachers for synthetic data. Pseudo-Reward Distillation partially removes the reward model but teacher inference remains costly.

Under-training vs. Chinchilla

Many small releases under-train relative to Chinchilla-optimal tokens; TinyLlama (3T) and MiniCPM WSD scheduler are exceptions, but the field is inconsistent.

Limited world knowledge

Small models forget long-tail facts faster than large ones; RAG helps but adds latency. Effective-parameter architectures (PLE in Gemma 4 E2B) are an emerging partial fix.

Benchmark insufficiency

MMLU / HellaSwag don't capture agentic / multimodal / sustainability behaviour. SLM-Bench, BFCL, ToolBench, IndicMMLU-Pro are newer but not universally adopted.

Sustainability not measured

Few papers report energy or CO₂ per 1k tokens. SLM-Bench is the exception; mainstream leaderboards still ignore it.

Medium-size FT plateaus

Khaliq & Patel show BART/PEGASUS/ProphetNet gain only marginally from SFT on summarization; not every domain rewards FT.

Multi-modality interference

Earlier multimodal SLMs exhibited cross-modal interference. Phi-4-Mini's Mixture-of-LoRAs mitigates it; scaling past 3 modalities (video, structured inputs) is unexplored.

Safety & interpretability

RL-induced CoT (DeepSeek-R1) is powerful but unaudited. "Artificial Entanglement" (2026) gives the first quantum-information-inspired lens on LoRA's structure, but rigorous interpretability is early.

Fine-tuning shifts toxicity unpredictably

DeepMind's 2024 study (The Effect of Fine-tuning on LM Toxicity) shows even non-adversarial LoRA FT can drastically alter Gemma/Llama/Phi toxicity — a major safety concern for production SLM fine-tuning.

Licensing constraints (pre-2026)

Pre-Gemma-4 Gemma/Llama licenses had MAU caps and restricted commercial fine-tuning. Gemma 4's Apache 2.0 release (Apr 2026) is the first frontier-class SLM with no such limits — the prior ecosystem was genuinely constrained.

Cross-lingual / low-resource gaps

Despite IndicLLMSuite, Airavata, Navarasa and MuRIL, Indic and African languages still lag English by a wide margin on both pretraining data and FT recipes.
