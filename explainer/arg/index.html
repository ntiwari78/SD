Hypothetical: The Coffee Spill Case

A customer orders a takeaway coffee. The lid is improperly secured. The coffee spills in the customer's car, causing burns and ruining the vehicle's interior. The coffee shop uses lids supplied by a third-party manufacturer.

Plaintiff's Arsenal

Duty of Care: The shop owes a duty to ensure products are safely assembled before serving.
Breach: The improperly secured lid is direct evidence of a breach — the shop failed to verify safety.
Causation: "But for" the unsecured lid, no spill would have occurred. The injury is a direct, foreseeable result.
Respondeat Superior: Even if an employee erred, the shop is vicariously liable for employee actions during employment.
