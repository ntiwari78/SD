The Prejudice Baked Into Language

The Bias Analogy
Why "left" became "wrong"

Imagine you live in a medieval village. Nine out of ten people are right-handed. So the village carpenter, the blacksmith, the surgeon — they all work with the right hand. That hand is skillful. It's trustworthy. It's normal.

Now someone walks in using their left hand. Strange. Different. Maybe suspicious. Over centuries, three languages independently coded this bias into vocabulary. Latin said dexter (right) = good, sinister (left) = ill-omened. French said droite (right) = adroit, gauche (left) = clumsy. English inherited all three.

This is the story of how social prejudice gets crystallised into root words — and how those roots then multiply into entire families of modern vocabulary.

The same multiplying logic works for hatred words (MISEIN) and marriage words (GAMOS). Once you see the root, every new combination becomes obvious.

The Marriage Taxonomy
Marriage words as a grid

Think of marriage vocabulary as a two-axis grid: how many spouses × which gender has the extra spouses.

MONO (one) + GAMOS = monogamy. POLY (many) + GAMOS = polygamy. POLY + GYNE (woman) = polygyny (one man, many wives). POLY + ANDRO (man) = polyandry (one woman, many husbands). Once you see the Latin/Greek components as tiles in a grid, you never need to memorise again — you just calculate.

The Hatred Prefix (MISEIN)
Hate as a prefix machine

MISEIN (to hate) is a Greek prefix factory. Attach it to any noun and you get someone who hates that thing. MISEIN + ANTHROPOS (mankind) = misanthropy. MISEIN + GYNE (woman) = misogyny. MISEIN + GAMOS (marriage) = misogamy. The logic is algebraic — learn the roots, calculate the word.

⊳ DEXTER Family (Latin: right hand)
DEXTER → dexterous
Skillful, especially with the hands. From the Latin right hand (the "good" hand).
DEXTER+-ITY → dexterity
Manual skill; adroitness; nimbleness of fingers or mind.
AMBI-+DEXTER+-OUS → ambidextrous
AMBI (both) + DEXTER (right) = "both hands are right hands." Able to use either hand with equal skill.
AMBI-+DEXTER+-ITY → ambidexterity
The noun form: the ability to use both hands equally well.
⊳ SINISTER / GAUCHE / ADROIT Family
SINISTER → sinister
Latin for "left." The left hand was considered unlucky in Roman augury (omens seen on the left = bad). Now means: evil, threatening, menacing.
GAUCHE → gauche
French for "left." Used in English to mean socially awkward or tactless — literally, "left-handed."
GAUCHE+-RIE → gaucherie
A socially awkward act; clumsiness in social situations. (Pronounced go′-shuh-ree)
À DROITE → adroit
French "à droite" = "to the right." Skilful, clever, deft. Especially of the mind.
ADROIT+-NESS → adroitness
Skill; cleverness; deftness; especially in intellectual or social matters.
⊳ ANTHROPOS Family (Greek: mankind)
ANTHROPOS+-LOGY → anthropology
The study (-logy) of mankind (anthropos). Covers human evolution, culture, social structures.
PHILEIN+ANTHROPOS → philanthropy
Love (philein) of mankind (anthropos). Active effort to promote human welfare through charitable giving.
MISEIN+ANTHROPOS → misanthropy
Hatred (misein) of mankind (anthropos). A general dislike and distrust of the human race.
⊳ GYNE Family (Greek: woman)
GYNE+-LOGY → gynecology
Study of woman (gyne) + (-logy). The branch of medicine dealing with diseases of the female reproductive system.
POLY-+GYNE → polygyny
Many (poly) wives (gyne). The custom where one man has multiple wives simultaneously.
MISEIN+GYNE → misogyny
Hatred (misein) of women (gyne). A deep-seated hostility or prejudice toward women.
⊳ GAMOS Family (Greek: marriage)
MONO-+GAMOS → monogamy
One (mono) marriage (gamos). The practice of having only one spouse at a time. Standard in Western societies.
BI-+GAMOS → bigamy
Two (bi) marriages (gamos). Being married to two people simultaneously — illegal in most countries.
POLY-+GAMOS → polygamy
Many (poly) marriages (gamos). The practice of having multiple spouses. Umbrella term for polygyny and polyandry.
POLY-+ANDRO → polyandry
Many (poly) husbands (andro). The custom of one woman having multiple husbands simultaneously. Rare in human societies.
MISEIN+GAMOS → misogamy
Hatred (misein) of marriage (gamos). Aversion to the institution of marriage itself (not to any person).
⊳ ASKETES (Greek: hermit, monk)
ASKETES+-ICISM → asceticism
The philosophy or practice of an asketes (a Greek monk who trained his body). Devotion to a severely simple, austere life — denying physical pleasures as a form of spiritual discipline.

Solecism
SOL′-uh-siz-um
An error in grammar, usage, or social behaviour — a violation of the accepted norms of speech or conduct.
Why this word? The Session 3 words cover social awkwardness (gauche, gaucherie) and skill (adroit, dexterous). A solecism is the linguistic cousin of gaucherie — a stumble in language or manners. Its root is the Greek city of Soloi, whose colonists supposedly spoke corrupt Athenian Greek. The city's name became a byword for linguistic barbarism.
