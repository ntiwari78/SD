I. Eat, Drink, and Be Merry

Latin vivo means to live. A convivium was a feast or banquet — literally, a "living together." From this root come words expressing liveliness, company, and the zest for existence.

II. The Egg: OVUM Family

Latin ovum means egg. It gives us a cluster of words relating to reproduction, shape, and female biology. Compare viviparous (live-bearing, from vivo) with oviparous (egg-laying, from ovum).

oviparous
ō-VIP'-ər-əs
Producing eggs that hatch outside the body (birds, reptiles, fish). From ovum + pareo (to bear).
oval / ovoid
Ō'-vəl / Ō'-void
Egg-shaped; oval means gently egg-shaped; ovoid (with suffix -oid = resembling) is more formally egg-shaped.
ovulate / ovulation
Ō'-vyə-layt' / ō-vyə-LAY'-shən
To release an egg from the ovary; the process of releasing an egg.
ovary / ovarian
Ō'-və-ree / ō-VAIR'-ee-ən
The female reproductive organ that produces eggs; adj. relating to the ovary.
fetus / fetal
FEE'-təs / FEE'-təl
A developing mammal in the womb, especially in its later stages; adj. relating to a fetus.
III. More About Life: VITA Family

Latin vita means life. From it come words of vitality, energy, and renewal — including the all-important word vitamin, literally a substance essential to life.

vital / vitality / vitalize / vitalization
VĪ'-təl / vī-TAL'-ə-tee / VĪ'-tə-līz' / vī'-tə-lə-ZAY'-shən
Essential to life; the state of being alive and vigorous; to give life to; the process of vitalizing.
revitalize / revitalization
ree-VĪ'-tə-līz'
To give new life and vigor to; the process of revitalizing.
devitalize / devitalization
dee-VĪ'-tə-līz'
To weaken; to deprive of vitality or strength; the process of devitalizing.
vitamin
VĪ'-tə-min
Any of several organic compounds essential for normal growth and nutrition. Literally "vital amine" — a substance of life.

V. Food and How to Enjoy (or Abuse) It
gourmand / gormandize
GōōR'-mənd / GAWR'-mən-dīz'
One who eats heartily or excessively; a lover of food (can be complimentary or slightly pejorative); to eat greedily and excessively.
glutton / gluttonous / gluttonize
GLUT'-ən / GLUT'-ə-nəs
One who eats and drinks excessively and without discrimination; habitually eating too much; to eat like a glutton.

Gourmet vs. Gourmand vs. Glutton:
A gourmet is discriminating — quality, not quantity.
A gourmand is enthusiastic — quantity is important, but quality matters too.
A glutton simply eats too much, with no particular discrimination.
The three form a spectrum from refined appreciation to mindless excess.

Food Vocabulary: A Spectrum
gourmet
gōōr-MAY'
A connoisseur of fine food and drink who values quality above all. A gourmet eats little but eats well.
gourmand
GōōR'-mənd
One who loves food and eats heartily. A gourmand values quality but also quantity — enthusiastic rather than ascetic. Related verb: gormandize.
glutton / gluttonous
GLUT'-ən / GLUT'-ə-nəs
One who eats and drinks to excess without discrimination. Purely about quantity, not quality.

Socratic Sandbox
On Vivisection

Vivisection — surgery on living animals for research — has long been controversial. Antivivisectionists argue that animal suffering is never justified by scientific gain. Defenders argue that medical advances depend on animal research. What framework would you use to decide when, if ever, such experiments are ethically permissible?

On Joie de Vivre vs. Ennui

The person with joie de vivre finds life endlessly interesting; the person with ennui finds it flat and pointless. Is ennui purely a psychological state, or can it be a cultural symptom — a sign of too much comfort and too little challenge? Can one cultivate joie de vivre, or is it a temperamental gift?

On the Food Spectrum

Consider: gourmet → gourmand → glutton. These three describe increasing quantity and decreasing discrimination. Society tends to celebrate the gourmet and pity the glutton. But is refinement of taste always a virtue? Or is there something pure — even honest — about the glutton's unreserved appetite? Where does passion for food end and self-indulgence begin?

On Vitality

The vita family gives us words for life, energy, and renewal. We speak of "revitalizing" cities, relationships, careers. What does it mean for something non-living to have vitality? When a city has vitality, what qualities does it actually possess? Is vitality observable, or is it more like beauty — entirely in the eye of the beholder?
