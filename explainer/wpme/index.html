Feynman Goal

We won't just memorize definitions. We will learn to see the Greek and Latin bones of every word you encounter.

The Habit of Vocabulary

Mental Model: Blueprints vs. Bricks

Imagine words are buildings. A dictionary gives you a list of every brick, but etymology gives you the blueprints. If you know the blueprint of "turning" (Verto), you understand every building that uses that brick—Introverts, Extroverts, and Ambiverts [cite: 31-41].

Socratic Dialogue

Q: If you learn "Pugnacious" from a dictionary, you might forget it. But if the root Pugnus means "Fist," you see a fighter. Why does your brain remember a "fist" better than a 10-letter definition?

Q: How does understanding a root allow you to guess the meaning of a word you've never seen before? [cite: 13-17]

Personality Types

Mental Model: The Mirror

The root Ego is Latin for "I". The Egoist keeps the mirror in their pocket; the Egotist forces you to look at the mirror with them [cite: 31-34].

Intro (Inward) + Verto (To Turn) = Introvert

Medical Specialists

Mental Model: The Map Station

A doctor's title is just a map coordinate. Derma is the "Skin" station. Ophthalmos is the "Eye" station [cite: 57-69].

Paidos (Child) + Iatreia (Healing) = Pediatrician

Socratic Challenge

Q: If a Pedagogue (Paidos + Agogos) is a leader of children, what is a Demagogue (Demos = People) doing to the crowd? [cite: 60-64]

Q: Why did the medical term "Orthopedist" originally refer to children if it now deals with adult bone structures? [cite: 63]

The Spectrum of Deception

Mental Model: The Building Height

A lie has different "heights." A Consummate liar has reached the "Summit" (Summus) of the art [cite: 147-152].

Notus (Known) + -ious = Notorious (Famous for bad reasons)

Decipher the Root

The root is Corrigio (To correct/set straight). The prefix is In- (Not).

Q: If a criminal is Incorrigible, what is the judge saying about the possibility of reform? [cite: 154-155]

Speech Habits

Mental Model: The Thrower

The root Loquor is the act of throwing words. A Soliloquy is throwing words at yourself while alone (Solus) [cite: 210-215].

Venter (Belly) + Loquor (Speak) = Ventriloquism

The Silent Challenge

The root Tacit means silent.

Q: If a person is Taciturn, are they likely to be Loquacious? Why is it impossible to be both at once? [cite: 205-207]
