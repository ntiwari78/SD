Etymology Family Tree 1: VENTER, DORSUM & Related Roots

venter/ventris (belly, womb) ├─ ventral (of the belly/front surface) ├─ ventricle (small chamber in heart/brain) │ └─ ventricular (relating to a ventricle) └─ endorse (to sign the back of; to approve) └─ endorsement (approval) dorsum (back) ├─ dorsal (of, on, or near the back) └─ endorse (to write on the back) auricula (little ear) └─ auricular (of or relating to the ear) avunculus (maternal uncle) └─ avuncular (uncle-like; kind and indulgent)

Etymology Family Tree 2: VOX/VOCIS + FERO & SOMNUS Families

vox/vocis (voice) + fero (to carry) └─ vociferous (loud, clamoring) ├─ vociferousness (quality of being vociferous) └─ vociferate (to cry out loudly; to shout) └─ vociferation (act of vociferating) fero/ferre (to carry, bear) + somnus (sleep) └─ somniferous (sleep-inducing) somnus (sleep) ├─ insomnia (inability to sleep) │ ├─ insomniac (one who has insomnia) │ └─ insomnious (relating to insomnia) ├─ somnolent (sleepy, drowsy) │ └─ somnolence (sleepiness) └─ somnambulism (sleepwalking) └─ somnambulist (sleepwalker)

Etymology Family Tree 3: AMBULO, SOPOR & ARTICULUS Families

ambulo (to walk) ├─ ambulatory (able to walk; covered walkway) ├─ perambulate (to walk through; to stroll) │ └─ perambulator (baby carriage; one who perambulates) ├─ amble (to walk leisurely) └─ preamble (introductory statement) sopor (deep sleep) └─ soporific (sleep-inducing; a sleep drug) articulus (small joint, knuckle) ├─ articulate (clear in speech; to express clearly) ├─ inarticulate (unable to express clearly) └─ articulation (act of expressing)

Becoming Alert to New Ideas

The Word Power Made Easy curriculum recommends the following works to expand your vocabulary and deepen your understanding of language:

The Art of Plain Talk
by Rudolf Flesch
A guide to clear, direct writing that emphasizes simplicity and precision in communication. Essential for understanding how word choice shapes meaning.
The Story of Language
by Mario Pei
A comprehensive history of language evolution, showing how words develop, migrate, and transform across cultures and centuries.
Words and Their Ways in English Speech
by Greenough and Kittredge
An exploration of etymology and word usage in English, demonstrating the historical roots and contemporary applications of vocabulary.
The American Language
by H.L. Mencken
A scholarly examination of American English and its unique characteristics, dialects, and departures from British English conventions.
Letter Writing
by Dr. Joyce Brothers
Practical guidance for writing letters with clarity, warmth, and appropriate vocabulary for different purposes and audiences.
