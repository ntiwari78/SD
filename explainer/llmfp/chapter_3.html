Chapter 03

The Writer: GPT & Autoregression

How simple probability at scale creates the illusion of consciousness.

✍️ The Core Intuition

Imagine an improvisational storyteller. They don't have a script; they only know the words they've already said. To decide the next word, they scan the entire history of the story and ask: "Given everything I've said so far, what is the most statistically likely word to come next?"

This is **Autoregression**. GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer) is a **Decoder-only** architecture. Unlike BERT, which looks at the future to understand the present, GPT is strictly "causal"—it is forbidden from seeing the future during training.

The Generative Loop

GPT works in a discrete loop. To generate a translation, it predicts one token, appends it to the input, and feeds the whole sequence back into itself.

1. Input

"The scroll says:"

2. Predict

Softmax([logits]) → "Truth"

3. Append

"The scroll says: Truth"

Causal Masking

To ensure the model doesn't "cheat" during training, we use a triangular mask in the attention mechanism. For any token $i$, it can only attend to tokens $j \le i$.

$$ \text{Mask}_{ij} = \begin{cases} 0 & \text{if } j \le i \\ -\infty & \text{if } j \gt i \end{cases} $$

Scaling: Why Bigger is Smarter

At OpenAI, we discovered the **Scaling Laws**. Model performance (Loss) follows a power-law relationship with three variables: Compute ($C$), Dataset Size ($D$), and Parameter Count ($N$).

$$ L(N, D) = \left( \frac{N_c}{N} \right)^{\alpha_N} + \left( \frac{D_c}{D} \right)^{\alpha_D} $$

As we scale $N$ and $D$, models stop just memorizing and start "reasoning." This is how GPT-4 became capable of zero-shot translation for languages it barely saw in its training set.

The Ancient Language Bridge: Generative Restoration

While BERT (Chapter 2) is great for "filling in blanks," GPT is superior for stylistic continuation.

If you have a half-finished prayer in an ancient language, GPT can continue the poem while maintaining the meter, tone, and religious context of the era. It acts as a bridge, hallucinating the most probable "cultural intent" of the original author to provide a fluent English translation.

The Mathematical Rigor

1. Directionality & Attention Matrices

BERT allows the hidden state $h_i$ to depend on the entire sequence $x$, whereas GPT restricts it to previous tokens.

$$ \text{BERT: } h_i = f(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_n) $$ $$ \text{GPT: } h_i = f(x_1, x_2, \dots, x_i) $$
Mathematically, GPT applies a causal mask $M$ to the attention weights $A$: $A_{ij} = -\infty$ for $j \gt i$.

2. Training Objectives (The Loss Functions)

BERT minimizes the cross-entropy of masked tokens $x_m$ given observed tokens $x_o$. GPT minimizes the negative log-likelihood of the next token.

$$ \mathcal{L}_{BERT} = -\sum_{x_m \in \text{Masked}} \log P(x_m \mid x_o) $$ $$ \mathcal{L}_{GPT} = -\sum_{t=1}^{T} \log P(x_t \mid x_{\lt t}) $$

3. Model Size & Scaling Complexity

While BERT models are typically "shallow" (e.g., 110M to 340M parameters), GPT models scale horizontally and vertically to reach "emergent" reasoning thresholds.

$$ \text{Inference Cost: } \mathcal{O}(L^2 \cdot d) \quad \text{vs.} \quad \mathcal{O}(L \cdot d \text{ with KV Caching}) $$
Decoders like GPT utilize KV Caching to reduce the generation cost from quadratic to linear relative to sequence length.

Hands-on: Autoregressive Sampling

Control the creativity of your generator using Temperature and Top-p (Nucleus) sampling.

from transformers import pipeline

# Use a standard GPT-2 or GPT-Neo model
generator = pipeline('text-generation', model='gpt2')

prompt = "The ancient inscription on the temple wall translates to:"

# Temperature: Higher = more creative, Lower = more predictable
# Top_p: Only consider the top 'p' percentage of probability mass
output = generator(
    prompt, 
    max_length=50, 
    temperature=0.7, 
    top_p=0.9, 
    num_return_sequences=1
)

print(output[0]['generated_text'])

Knowledge Check

1. What happens if we set Temperature $T = 0$?
The model becomes deterministic. It will always pick the token with the highest logit. Mathematically, as $T \to 0$, the softmax distribution collapses into a one-hot vector (Argmax). $$ P_i = \frac{\exp(z_i / T)}{\sum \exp(z_j / T)} $$
2. Why do GPT models sometimes "hallucinate"?
GPT is a stochastic parrot. It doesn't have a database of facts; it has a database of probabilities. If the most "statistically likely" next word is a lie, it will say it with total confidence. This is why for ancient translations, we must ground GPT with a "Reader" like BERT or a Vector Database.
